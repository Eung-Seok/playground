Domination Convergence Theorem Let {$f$n} be a sequence measurable functions. Suppose that the sequence converges pointwise to a function $f$ i.e.

\[\lim_{n \to \infty} f_n(x) = f(x) \]

exists for every $x \in S$. Asuume that the sequence $f_n$ is dominated by some integrable function $g$such that \[ \left|f_n(x) \right| \leq g(x)\] for all point $x \in S$ and all $n$ in the index set. Then $f_n, f$ are integrable, and \[ \lim_{n \to \infty} \int_{S} f_n d\mu = \int_{S} \lim_{n \to \infty} f_nd\mu = \int_{S} fd\mu\]


Theorem 3.2.1 Green's Theorem

Let C be a positive oriented, piecewise smooth, simple closed curve, in a plane, and $D$ be the region bounded by $C$. If $L$ and $M$ are functions of $(x, y)$ defined on an open region, open region containing $D$ and have continuous partial derivatives there, then \[ \oint\limits_{C} \big(Ldx + Mdy \big) = \iint_{D} \bigg( \frac{\partial M}{\partial x} - \frac{\partial L}{\partial y} \bigg)dA\] where the path of integration along $C$ is counterclockwise.
